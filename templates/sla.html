Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Last updated: January 1, 2025

1. Overview

This Service Level Agreement ("SLA") defines the performance standards and commitments that SilentCanary provides to its customers. This SLA is governed by Ontario, Canada law and applies to all paid subscription plans. Customers located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada are subject to local consumer protection laws.

2. Service Availability Commitments

2.1 Uptime Guarantees by Plan

2.2 Service Components Covered

This SLA covers the following service components:

  • Web Application: SilentCanary dashboard and user interface
  • API Services: REST API for canary management and monitoring
  • Monitoring System: Canary check-in processing and status tracking
  • Alert System: Email, SMS, and webhook notifications
  • Data Storage: Canary configurations and historical data

  • Canary Status Updates: Processed within 30 seconds of check-in
  • Alert Triggering: Initiated within 1 minute of missed check-in
  • Dashboard Updates: Real-time updates within 10 seconds
  • Historical Data: Available for minimum 12 months

5. Exclusions

This SLA does not apply to service interruptions caused by:

  • Scheduled maintenance (announced 24 hours in advance)
  • Customer's internet connectivity issues
  • Third-party service outages (AWS, Stripe, etc.)
  • Force majeure events (natural disasters, etc.)
  • Customer misuse or violation of Terms of Service
  • DDoS attacks or other malicious activities
  • Customer's failure to follow recommended configurations

6. Service Credits

6.1 Credit Calculation

Service credits are calculated as a percentage of the monthly subscription fee based on the actual uptime achieved:

  • Credits are applied automatically to the next billing cycle
  • Credits cannot exceed 100% of the monthly fee
  • Credits do not extend service terms
  • Unused credits do not carry over beyond the next billing period

6.2 Credit Request Process

To request service credits:

  1. Submit a support ticket within 30 days of the incident
  2. Provide specific dates and times of service disruption
  3. Include any relevant error messages or screenshots
  4. Credits will be reviewed and applied within 30 days

7. Monitoring and Reporting

SilentCanary continuously monitors service performance using:

  • Automated health checks every 30 seconds
  • Real-time performance monitoring
  • Third-party uptime monitoring services
  • Customer-reported incidents

7.1 Status Page

Current service status and incident history is available at our Status Page, which is updated in real-time.

8. Incident Response

Our incident response process includes:

  1. Detection: Automated monitoring identifies issues within 1 minute
  2. Response: On-call engineers notified immediately
  3. Communication: Status page updated within 5 minutes
  4. Resolution: Team works to restore service as quickly as possible
  5. Post-mortem: Root cause analysis for significant incidents

9. Data Backup and Recovery

Data protection measures include:

  • Automated daily backups with 30-day retention
  • Cross-region data replication
  • Point-in-time recovery capabilities
  • Regular backup integrity testing
  • Maximum data loss: 1 hour (RPO)
  • Maximum recovery time: 4 hours (RTO)

10. Security Commitments

Security measures include:

  • 256-bit SSL/TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • AES-256 encryption for data at rest
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • 24/7 security monitoring

11. SLA Modifications

SilentCanary reserves the right to modify this SLA with 30 days' written notice. Modifications will not reduce the service levels during any current subscription term.
